Car Showcase - Audi 100 C3

Audi 100 C3

Year: 1982-1991

Engine Options:

  • 1.8L I4 Petrol (75-90 hp)
  • 2.0L I5 Petrol (100 hp)
  • 2.1L I5 Petrol (115 hp)
  • 2.2L I5 Petrol (115-138 hp)
  • 2.3L I5 Petrol (136 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Diesel (69 hp)
  • 2.4L I5 Diesel (82 hp)
  • 2.5L I5 Diesel (120 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed automatic
  • 5-speed manual

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 130 mph (209 km/h) for the 2.2 Turbo model
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 8.5 seconds for the 2.2 Turbo model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,798 mm (188.9 in)
  • Width: 1,814 mm (71.4 in)
  • Height: 1,417 mm (55.8 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,693 mm (106.0 in)
  • Weight: 1,150-1,400 kg (2,535-3,086 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Audi 100 C3 was known for its aerodynamic design, featuring a drag coefficient of just 0.30, which was exceptional for its time.
  • Innovative galvanized body panels that resisted corrosion, contributing to the car's durability.
  • A spacious and comfortable interior with high-quality materials and advanced features for its era, such as air conditioning and electric windows.
  • Available with Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system, providing superior traction and handling in various driving conditions.
  • Distinctive flush door handles, which reduced drag and added to the car's sleek appearance.

Notable Variants:

  • 100 Avant: The wagon version of the Audi 100, offering additional cargo space while maintaining the aerodynamic design.
  • 100 Quattro: The all-wheel-drive version of the Audi 100, equipped with the Quattro system, enhancing performance and stability.
  • 200 Turbo: A high-performance variant featuring a 2.2L turbocharged engine, producing up to 200 hp, and additional sporty features.

Historical Significance:

The Audi 100 C3 marked a significant step forward in automotive design, particularly in aerodynamics and corrosion resistance. Its innovative approach influenced the design of future Audi models and set new standards in the automotive industry. The C3 was well-regarded for its reliability, build quality, and advanced engineering, making it a popular choice in both Europe and North America.

Legacy:

The Audi 100 C3 is considered a classic today, appreciated for its groundbreaking design and technological advancements. It played a key role in establishing Audi as a premium automotive brand, known for innovation and quality. The C3's influence can still be seen in modern Audi vehicles, particularly in their emphasis on aerodynamics and engineering excellence.
