Car Showcase - Toyota Camry V20

Toyota Camry V20

Year: 1986-1990

Engine Options:

  • 1.8L I4 Petrol (79 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (92-120 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Diesel (85 hp)
  • 2.5L V6 Petrol (156 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed automatic
  • 5-speed manual

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 118 mph (190 km/h) for the 2.5L V6 model
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 9.2 seconds for the 2.5L V6 model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,470 mm (176.0 in)
  • Width: 1,695 mm (66.7 in)
  • Height: 1,395 mm (54.9 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,600 mm (102.4 in)
  • Weight: 1,090-1,310 kg (2,403-2,888 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Toyota Camry V20 introduced a more streamlined and modern design compared to its predecessor, emphasizing practicality and reliability.
  • Known for its durability, the Camry V20 featured a well-built interior with comfortable seating and a user-friendly layout.
  • Available in both sedan and station wagon body styles, catering to a wide range of customer needs.
  • Offered with a range of features, including air conditioning, power windows, and optional anti-lock brakes (ABS) on higher trims.
  • The V20 generation marked the first appearance of the Camry in a V6 configuration, offering improved performance and smoothness.

Notable Variants:

  • Camry V6: The first Camry to feature a V6 engine, providing enhanced power and refinement.
  • Camry All-Trac: An all-wheel-drive variant designed for better traction and handling in adverse conditions.
  • Camry Wagon: A practical station wagon version, offering increased cargo space and versatility.

Historical Significance:

The Toyota Camry V20 solidified the Camry's reputation as a reliable, durable, and practical family car. Its introduction of a V6 engine and all-wheel-drive options expanded its appeal, making it a strong competitor in the global automotive market. The V20 generation was a significant contributor to Toyota's growing dominance in the mid-size sedan segment.

Legacy:

The Camry V20 is remembered as one of the key models that established Toyota's reputation for building long-lasting, dependable vehicles. It laid the foundation for the Camry's success in subsequent generations, eventually becoming one of the best-selling cars in the world.
