Car Showcase - Peugeot 405

Peugeot 405

Year: 1987-1997

Engine Options:

  • 1.6L I4 Petrol (92 hp)
  • 1.9L I4 Petrol (110-158 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (123-160 hp)
  • 1.9L I4 Diesel (70-92 hp)
  • 1.8L I4 Diesel (60 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed automatic
  • 5-speed manual

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 137 mph (220 km/h) for the Mi16 model
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 7.9 seconds for the Mi16 model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,410 mm (173.6 in)
  • Width: 1,710 mm (67.3 in)
  • Height: 1,390 mm (54.7 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,660 mm (104.7 in)
  • Weight: 1,000-1,200 kg (2,205-2,646 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Peugeot 405 was recognized for its sleek, modern design that earned it several awards, including the European Car of the Year in 1988.
  • It featured a comfortable, well-built interior with ample space for passengers and luggage, making it ideal for families.
  • The car was available in both sedan and estate (station wagon) versions, catering to various customer needs.
  • Its advanced suspension system provided a smooth, stable ride, contributing to its reputation for excellent handling.
  • The Mi16 variant was particularly celebrated for its high performance and sporty feel.

Notable Variants:

  • 405 Mi16: A performance model equipped with a 1.9L 16-valve engine, delivering up to 160 hp and enhanced handling characteristics.
  • 405 Estate: The station wagon version, known for its practicality and extensive cargo space.
  • 405 T16: A rare turbocharged version with all-wheel drive, producing up to 200 hp, and featuring rally-inspired design elements.

Qualities:

  • Comfort: The Peugeot 405 was highly praised for its comfortable ride, offering a spacious and refined cabin that appealed to families and long-distance travelers.
  • Handling: Its precise steering and well-balanced suspension made it a joy to drive, especially in its sportier variants like the Mi16.
  • Versatility: With both sedan and estate options, and a variety of engines, the 405 catered to a wide range of drivers, from those seeking fuel efficiency to performance enthusiasts.
  • Design: The 405's aerodynamic shape and stylish design were ahead of their time, making it a visually appealing choice that aged gracefully.
  • Durability: Known for its solid build quality, the Peugeot 405 was a reliable vehicle that often remained in service for many years.

Historical Significance:

The Peugeot 405 was a critical model for the brand, helping to establish Peugeot's reputation for building cars that were both stylish and practical. It was one of the best-selling cars of its time and played a significant role in Peugeot's global success.

Legacy:

Today, the Peugeot 405 is remembered as a classic of the late 1980s and early 1990s, particularly in its Mi16 form, which remains popular among enthusiasts. The 405 set a standard for future Peugeot models, particularly in terms of ride comfort and handling.
