BMW 3 Series E30 - Full Information

Overview

  • Manufacturer: BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke AG)
  • Production Years: 1982 - 1994
  • Assembly: Munich, Germany; Regensburg, Germany; Rosslyn, South Africa
  • Designer: Claus Luthe, Boyke Boyer

Body Styles

  • 2-Door Sedan (Coupe)
  • 4-Door Sedan
  • Convertible (Cabriolet)
  • Touring (Wagon) - Introduced in 1987

Notable Models and Trims

  • 318i/318is: Entry-level models featuring 1.8L M10/M40 inline-four engines.
  • 320i/320is: Mid-range models with 2.0L M20 inline-six engines.
  • 325e/325es: Models focused on fuel efficiency, equipped with a 2.7L M20 "eta" engine.
  • 325i/325is: Performance-oriented models with a 2.5L M20 inline-six engine.
  • M3: The high-performance variant, featuring a 2.3L S14 inline-four engine, and was a successful model in touring car racing.

Engine Specifications

  • M10 Inline-Four Engine: 1.8L, 90-105 hp
  • M40 Inline-Four Engine: 1.6L - 1.8L, 102 hp
  • M20 Inline-Six Engine: 2.0L - 2.7L, 120 hp - 170 hp
  • S14 Inline-Four Engine: 2.3L, 192-238 hp (M3)

Transmission Options

  • 4-Speed Manual
  • 5-Speed Manual
  • 3-Speed Automatic
  • 4-Speed Automatic

Chassis and Suspension

  • Front Suspension: MacPherson struts with anti-roll bar
  • Rear Suspension: Semi-trailing arm independent suspension
  • Brakes: Disc brakes on all models, with ventilated discs on higher-performance models like the 325i and M3.

Performance

  • 0-60 mph (0-100 km/h): 6.5 seconds (M3)
  • Top Speed: 146 mph (235 km/h) (M3)

Dimensions

  • Wheelbase: 2,570 mm (101.2 in)
  • Length: 4,335 mm (170.7 in) - Sedan
  • Width: 1,645 mm (64.8 in)
  • Height: 1,380 mm (54.3 in) - Sedan
  • Curb Weight: 1,100 - 1,300 kg (2,425 - 2,866 lbs) depending on the model

Notable Features

  • Interior: Ergonomically designed, with a driver-focused dashboard. The higher trims featured options like leather seats, air conditioning, and premium sound systems.
  • Technology: Some models were equipped with ABS, power steering, and electronic fuel injection, which were advanced for the time.
  • Motorsport Pedigree: The E30 M3 was highly successful in touring car racing, winning numerous championships in the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Legacy

The BMW E30 is highly regarded among enthusiasts for its balance of performance, handling, and durability. It has become a classic, with the M3 variant being especially sought after by collectors.
