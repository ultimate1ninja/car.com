Car Showcase - Nissan Maxima 1981

Nissan Maxima 1981

Year: 1981

Engine Options:

  • 2.4L I6 Petrol (110 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed manual
  • 3-speed automatic

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Approximately 110 mph (177 km/h)
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 10 seconds

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,480 mm (176.4 in)
  • Width: 1,800 mm (70.9 in)
  • Height: 1,410 mm (55.5 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,670 mm (105.1 in)
  • Weight: 1,250 kg (2,755 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The 1981 Nissan Maxima was noted for its solid and reliable build, featuring a sleek, aerodynamic design for its time.
  • It offered a spacious interior with comfortable seating and a well-laid-out dashboard.
  • Standard features included air conditioning, power windows, and an AM/FM radio.
  • The Maxima was equipped with front disc brakes and rear drum brakes, providing adequate stopping power.

Notable Variants:

  • Maxima GXE: The base model with standard features and equipment.
  • Maxima SE: A sportier variant with additional features and improved suspension.

Historical Significance:

The 1981 Nissan Maxima was a significant model for Nissan as it marked the beginning of the Maxima's evolution into a more refined and sporty vehicle. It helped establish Nissan's reputation for producing reliable and well-engineered sedans in the American market.

Legacy:

The 1981 Maxima is remembered as a solid, reliable, and practical vehicle. Its introduction paved the way for future models that continued to build on its reputation for quality and performance. The Maxima remains a symbol of Nissan's commitment to producing durable and well-designed vehicles.
