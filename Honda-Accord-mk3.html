Car Showcase - Honda Accord Mk3

Honda Accord Mk3

Year: 1985-1989

Engine Options:

  • 1.6L I4 Petrol (80 hp)
  • 1.8L I4 Petrol (98 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (100-122 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol DOHC (138 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed automatic
  • 5-speed manual

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 118 mph (190 km/h)
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 9.8 seconds for the 2.0L DOHC model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,600 mm (181.1 in)
  • Width: 1,695 mm (66.7 in)
  • Height: 1,365 mm (53.7 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,615 mm (102.9 in)
  • Weight: 1,000-1,160 kg (2,205-2,557 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Honda Accord Mk3 was known for its sleek, modern design with pop-up headlights, a hallmark of the 1980s era.
  • Offered advanced features for its time, such as a digital dashboard and cruise control in higher trims.
  • The Mk3 was available in various body styles, including sedan, coupe, and hatchback, catering to diverse customer needs.
  • Featured Honda's innovative double-wishbone suspension system, providing superior handling and ride comfort.
  • Interior design focused on ergonomics, with easy-to-reach controls and high-quality materials.

Notable Variants:

  • Accord Aerodeck: A 3-door shooting-brake version with a distinctive rear hatch design, offering a unique blend of style and practicality.
  • Accord Si: A sportier variant equipped with the 2.0L DOHC engine, offering enhanced performance and handling.
  • Accord EX: A higher trim level with additional luxury features such as leather upholstery, sunroof, and premium audio system.

Historical Significance:

The Honda Accord Mk3 played a significant role in establishing Honda as a global automotive leader. It was praised for its reliability, innovative features, and exceptional build quality, contributing to its popularity worldwide. The Mk3 also introduced design elements that influenced future Accord models.

Legacy:

Today, the Honda Accord Mk3 is considered a classic, especially among enthusiasts who appreciate its blend of technology, design, and driving dynamics. Its influence can be seen in subsequent Accord generations, particularly in the emphasis on quality and innovation that Honda continues to uphold.
