Car Showcase - Mercedes-Benz 190E

Mercedes-Benz 190E

Year: 1982-1993

Engine Options:

  • 1.8L I4 Petrol (102 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (118-122 hp)
  • 2.3L I4 Petrol (136-185 hp)
  • 2.5L I4 Petrol (195 hp)
  • 2.6L I6 Petrol (158-160 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Diesel (72 hp)
  • 2.5L I5 Diesel (90-94 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed automatic
  • 5-speed manual

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 143 mph (230 km/h) for the 2.5-16 Cosworth model
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 7.5 seconds for the 2.3-16 Cosworth model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,445 mm (175.0 in)
  • Width: 1,679 mm (66.1 in)
  • Height: 1,380 mm (54.3 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,665 mm (104.9 in)
  • Weight: 1,160-1,290 kg (2,557-2,844 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • Designed by Bruno Sacco, featuring a compact, aerodynamic shape.
  • Innovative multi-link rear suspension, offering superior handling and ride comfort.
  • High-quality interior with optional leather seats, wood trim, and advanced climate control.
  • Available with optional ABS, airbags, and power accessories.
  • The 190E 2.3-16 and 2.5-16 models featured sportier styling, a body kit, and a limited-slip differential.

Notable Variants:

  • 190E 2.3-16 (1984-1988): Developed in collaboration with Cosworth, this model was designed for high performance, with a 2.3L I4 engine producing 185 hp. It was used in motorsport, including DTM racing.
  • 190E 2.5-16 (1988-1993): A further development of the 2.3-16, featuring a larger 2.5L engine and increased power output to 195 hp. Known for its success in touring car championships.
  • Evolution I (1989): A limited-edition homologation special with a larger rear wing, flared wheel arches, and suspension upgrades, designed for Group A racing.
  • Evolution II (1990): An even more aggressive version with a 2.5L engine, producing 235 hp. It featured aerodynamic enhancements, including a large rear wing and deeper front air dam. Only 502 units were produced.

Historical Significance:

The Mercedes-Benz 190E, particularly the Cosworth-engineered variants, played a crucial role in Mercedes-Benz's motorsport history. It proved that the brand could compete in the performance sedan market, leading to the development of future AMG models. The 190E was also known for its durability and was widely regarded as over-engineered, making it a popular choice for both everyday driving and motorsports.

Legacy:

The Mercedes-Benz 190E is often considered the precursor to the modern C-Class, laying the foundation for Mercedes-Benz's compact executive car lineup. Its combination of luxury, performance, and engineering excellence has made it a classic, with well-preserved examples highly sought after by collectors today.
