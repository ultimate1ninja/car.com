Car Showcase - Renault 21

Renault 21

Year: 1986-1994

Engine Options:

  • 1.4L I4 Petrol (60 hp)
  • 1.7L I4 Petrol (73-90 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (115 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Turbo Petrol (162 hp)
  • 1.9L I4 Diesel (64 hp)
  • 2.1L I4 Turbo Diesel (88 hp)

Transmission:

  • 5-speed manual
  • 4-speed automatic

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 137 mph (220 km/h) for the Turbo model
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 7.6 seconds for the Turbo model

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,470 mm (176.0 in)
  • Width: 1,696 mm (66.8 in)
  • Height: 1,390 mm (54.7 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,670 mm (105.1 in)
  • Weight: 1,050-1,300 kg (2,315-2,866 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Renault 21 featured a conservative yet practical design with a spacious interior, making it a popular family car in Europe.
  • Available in various body styles, including sedan, hatchback, and estate, the Renault 21 offered versatility for different customer needs.
  • It was known for its comfortable ride and efficient engines, especially in the diesel variants, which were highly economical.
  • The Renault 21 Turbo was the high-performance model, recognized for its impressive acceleration and top speed.

Notable Variants:

  • Renault 21 Turbo: The performance-oriented version with a 2.0L turbocharged engine, offering up to 162 hp, and sporty styling cues.
  • Renault 21 Nevada: The estate (station wagon) version, providing ample cargo space and practicality for families.
  • Renault 21 Quadra: A four-wheel-drive variant, enhancing traction and handling in various driving conditions.

Qualities:

  • Versatility: With a range of body styles and engine options, the Renault 21 catered to various customer preferences, from family-friendly estates to sporty sedans.
  • Comfort: The Renault 21 was praised for its smooth ride and well-insulated cabin, making it a comfortable choice for long journeys.
  • Economy: Especially in its diesel variants, the Renault 21 was known for its fuel efficiency, making it an economical option for budget-conscious drivers.
  • Performance: The Turbo model added a sporty edge to the lineup, offering thrilling performance while maintaining everyday usability.
  • Reliability: Renault built the 21 with a focus on durability, contributing to its reputation as a dependable vehicle.

Historical Significance:

The Renault 21 played a key role in Renault's lineup during the late 1980s and early 1990s, competing effectively in the European mid-size car market. It was well-regarded for its combination of practicality, comfort, and performance, particularly in the Turbo variant.

Legacy:

Today, the Renault 21 is remembered as a solid, reliable vehicle that offered a range of options for different drivers. Its Turbo version remains a favorite among enthusiasts for its blend of performance and practicality.
