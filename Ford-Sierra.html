Car Showcase - Ford Sierra

Ford Sierra

Year: 1982-1993

Engine Options:

  • 1.3L I4 Petrol (60 hp)
  • 1.6L I4 Petrol (75-105 hp)
  • 1.8L I4 Petrol (90 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Petrol (100-115 hp)
  • 2.3L V6 Petrol (114-135 hp)
  • 2.8L V6 Petrol (150-160 hp)
  • 2.0L I4 Diesel (67 hp)
  • 1.8L I4 Diesel (75 hp)

Transmission:

  • 4-speed manual
  • 5-speed manual
  • 3-speed automatic
  • 4-speed automatic

Performance:

  • Top Speed: Up to 135 mph (217 km/h) for the Sierra RS Cosworth
  • 0-60 mph: Approximately 6.5 seconds for the Sierra RS Cosworth

Dimensions:

  • Length: 4,453 mm (175.3 in)
  • Width: 1,723 mm (67.8 in)
  • Height: 1,401 mm (55.1 in)
  • Wheelbase: 2,608 mm (102.6 in)
  • Weight: 1,000-1,300 kg (2,205-2,866 lbs)

Design and Features:

  • The Ford Sierra was known for its aerodynamic design, which was a significant departure from the boxy designs of its predecessors.
  • It featured a rear-wheel-drive layout, providing a balanced driving experience.
  • The Sierra was available in various body styles, including a 3-door hatchback, 5-door hatchback, estate (wagon), and saloon (sedan).
  • The car was popular for its spacious interior, making it a practical family car.
  • Advanced features for its time, such as electronic fuel injection, anti-lock brakes (on higher-end models), and a digital dashboard in some variants.

Notable Variants:

  • Sierra XR4i: A sporty version featuring a 2.8L V6 engine, unique body styling, and rear-wheel drive.
  • Sierra RS Cosworth: A high-performance version with a turbocharged 2.0L I4 engine, producing up to 204 hp, and a rear spoiler for enhanced aerodynamics.
  • Sierra Sapphire: A more luxurious version with enhanced comfort features, available in both 4-door saloon and estate versions.

Historical Significance:

The Ford Sierra played a significant role in Ford's lineup during the 1980s and early 1990s. Its aerodynamic design set a new standard for family cars and influenced automotive design trends. The Sierra RS Cosworth, in particular, became a motorsport icon, dominating touring car championships and earning a strong following among enthusiasts.

Legacy:

Today, the Ford Sierra is remembered as a classic car that combined practicality with performance. The Sierra RS Cosworth, in particular, is highly sought after by collectors and remains a symbol of Ford's racing heritage. The Sierra's design and engineering principles continue to influence modern Ford vehicles.
